---
title: "Connect Your Calendar"
description: "Link your Microsoft Outlook calendar so Fano can see your schedule and join meetings automatically."
---

Connecting your calendar is what makes Fano Summary hands-free. Once linked, Fano sees your upcoming meetings and sends the note-taker bot to join each call automatically.

## How to connect

1. Click your **profile avatar** in the top-right corner of the app.
2. Select **Setting**.
3. Go to the **Integration** tab.
4. Find **Microsoft Outlook** and click **Connect**.
5. Sign in with your Microsoft account and grant calendar access.

Once connected, you'll see a green **"Connected"** badge next to Microsoft Outlook.

<Info>
Fano only reads your calendar to detect meeting times and join links. It does not modify your calendar events.
</Info>

## What happens after connecting

- Fano scans your calendar for meetings with video call links (Teams, Zoom, or Google Meet).
- When a scheduled meeting starts, the Fano bot joins automatically — visible to all participants as a note-taker.
- Meetings appear in your Fano Summary dashboard, organized by date.

## Disconnecting your calendar

If you need to disconnect:

1. Go to **Settings → Integration**.
2. Click the **three-dot menu (⋯)** next to Microsoft Outlook.
3. Select **Disconnect**.

After disconnecting, Fano will no longer auto-join your meetings. You can still use **Record Now** or **Upload Meeting** to capture meetings manually.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I don't see my meetings after connecting">
    Make sure your meetings have a video call link (Teams, Zoom, or Google Meet) in the calendar event. Fano can only join meetings that include a join link.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Connection failed or stuck on loading">
    Try disconnecting and reconnecting. If the issue persists, check that your Microsoft account has the required permissions and that pop-ups are not blocked in your browser.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I connect Google Calendar?">
    Fano Summary currently supports Microsoft Outlook. Google Calendar support is on our roadmap.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
